About Mershon Enterprises

Mershon Enterprises was founded February 2015, but has roots that tie back to summer of 2003. Kevin Mershon, a Bakersfield native, started his career as a software developmer at Krummrich Engineering in the Metro Centre off Truxton Ave. at the age of 18. Over the course of the next ten years, Kevin learned the ins and outs of the petroleum, shipping, finance, and automation industries, trained over a dozen developers as apprentices, and designed and maintained at least as many software systems.

Today, Mershon Enterprises is transitioning into a "Platform as a Service" company, though we still provide a a lot of ongoing support and generalized custom software development to clients local and afar.

Meet the Team

Kevin Mershon

Founder, President

Kevin Mershon is a 31-year-old software developer, tech enthusiast, and entrepreneur. He identifies primarily as a back-end developer, and is currently a self-proclaimed fanboy of Clojure. He founded Mershon Enterprises as his third entrepreneurial venture, and employs several other software developers. He was instrumental in helping to found Mesh Cowork, Bakersfield's first collaborative shared working space and KIT: Kern Innovation & Technology community in assistance to Alyssa Haas, and is personally driven to see Bakersfield grow into a hub of technology innovation.

Alyssa Haas

Business Development Manager

Alyssa is a Stanford and UCLA alumna with a diversity of experiences in technology from Los Angeles to Silicon Valley to Moscow. She specializes in startup communities and business development. She also loves to mentor for the Bakersfield and Kern County tech communities as well as internationally and for her alma maters.

In 2016, with the proud support and backing of Mershon Enterprises, Alyssa founded KIT, the Kern Innovation & Technology Community. This community seeks to support and promote innovation and technology in Kern County in a movement led by entrepreneurs. Together, so far we have hosted a drawing contest for kids, funded a 4th grade teacher's class to get educational robots, and hosted Bakersfield's first three public hackathons: "Distill Your Idea", "Tech Fighting the Wildfires", and "Electric Bookworm", each with more attendance and community engagement than the previous. To learn more, check out followthefox.org.

Who are we?

The roots of Mershon Enterprises date back to summer 2003, though the company in its current incarnation was founded April 2015. Kevin Mershon, a Bakersfield native, started his career as a freelance software developmer at Krummrich Engineering, a Bakersfield oilfield engineering company. Over the course of the next ten years, Kevin learned the ins and outs of the petroleum and shipping industries, trained over a dozen developers as apprentices, and designed and maintained at least as many software systems.

Kevin Mershon

Founder, President

Kevin Mershon is a 30-year-old software developer, tech enthusiast, and entrepreneur. He identifies primarily as a back-end developer, and is currently a self-proclaimed fanboy of Clojure. He recently founded Mershon Enterprises as his third startup in early 2015, and employs several other software developers. He was instrumental in helping to found Mesh Cowork, Bakersfield's first collaborative shared working space located downtown on 20th and Eye Street, and is personally driven to see Bakersfield grow into a hub of technology innovation.

Alyssa Haerle

Business Development Manager

Alyssa is a Stanford and UCLA alumna with a diversity of experiences in technology from Los Angeles to Silicon Valley to Moscow. She specializes in startup communities and business development. She also loves to mentor for the Bakersfield and Kern County tech communities as well as internationally and for her alma maters.

In 2016, with the proud support and backing of Mershon Enterprises, Alyssa founded KIT, the Kern Innovation & Technology Community. This community seeks to support and promote innovation and technology in Kern County in a movement led by entrepreneurs. Together, so far we have hosted a drawing contest for kids, funded a 4th grade teacher's class to get educational robots, and hosted Bakersfield's first public hackathon, "Distill Your Idea". To learn more, check out followthefox.org.

Bethany Armitage

Software Developer

Since then she's made phenomenal progress developing programming skills by a combination of what she can teach herself, what she can learn from senior developers, and by pursuing a bachelors degree in computer science.
